# Understanding Construction Timelines and What to Expect

One of the most common questions we hear from clients is, "How long will it take to build my home?" The honest answer depends on the size, complexity, and site conditions of your project, but understanding the major phases helps set realistic expectations and keeps the experience enjoyable.

**Pre-construction (2-4 months):** This phase includes architectural design, engineering, permitting, and selections. It is tempting to rush through this stage, but the time spent here directly affects how smoothly construction goes. Finalizing your floor plan, choosing materials, and locking in allowances before breaking ground prevents costly change orders later. We guide clients through a structured selections process so nothing falls through the cracks.

**Site work and foundation (3-6 weeks):** Once permits are in hand, the lot is cleared, utilities are trenched, and the foundation is formed and poured. In the Hill Country, this phase can vary significantly depending on rock conditions. A flat lot with deep soil might take three weeks. A sloped lot on solid limestone could take six weeks or more due to the excavation required.

**Framing through dry-in (6-10 weeks):** This is when your home begins to take shape. Framing goes up, the roof is installed, and windows and exterior doors are set. By the end of this phase, the house is "dried in" -- protected from weather -- and you can walk through the actual spaces for the first time. This is one of the most exciting milestones in the build.

**Mechanical, finish, and detail work (10-16 weeks):** Electrical, plumbing, and HVAC rough-ins happen first, followed by insulation, drywall, cabinetry, flooring, tile, paint, and fixtures. This is the longest phase and where your selections come to life. We schedule weekly client walk-throughs during this period so you can see progress and address questions in real time.

**Final inspections and closing (2-3 weeks):** Punch list items are completed, final inspections are passed, and we do a thorough walkthrough with you before handing over the keys.
