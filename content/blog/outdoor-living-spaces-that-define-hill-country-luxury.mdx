# Outdoor Living Spaces That Define Hill Country Luxury

In the Texas Hill Country, some of the best living happens outside. With over 260 days of sunshine per year and cool evenings that stretch from spring through fall, outdoor spaces are not a bonus feature -- they are essential to the way people live here. The best custom homes treat outdoor areas with the same level of design and craftsmanship as any interior room.

A covered patio with a full outdoor kitchen is the centerpiece of most Hill Country outdoor living designs. We are not talking about a grill on a concrete slab. We mean a properly designed space with a built-in smoker, gas burners, refrigeration, a sink with hot and cold water, and bar seating for guests. Natural stone countertops and a wood-burning or gas fireplace complete the space. When done right, you can prepare an entire meal outdoors without stepping inside once.

Pool design in the Hill Country should respond to the landscape. Infinity-edge pools that appear to spill into the valley below are a dramatic choice on sloped lots. For flatter terrain, a freeform pool with a natural stone surround and integrated spa creates a resort feel. We always design the pool and patio together so that materials, elevations, and sight lines are cohesive rather than feeling like separate projects bolted together.

Shade is critical in Texas. Deep roof overhangs, pergolas with motorized louvers, and strategically placed shade trees allow you to use outdoor spaces comfortably even in July and August. Ceiling fans rated for outdoor use and misting systems add another layer of comfort during peak heat.

Do not forget about evening enjoyment. A fire pit with built-in seating, landscape lighting that highlights specimen trees and architectural features, and a sound system with weather-rated speakers make your outdoor spaces just as inviting after dark as they are during the day.
