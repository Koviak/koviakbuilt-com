# Building Near Water: Guadalupe River Properties

There are few things more desirable in the Texas Hill Country than owning property along the Guadalupe River. The sound of water moving over limestone, the shade of ancient cypress trees, and the sheer beauty of the river corridor make these sites some of the most sought-after in the region. But building near water requires careful planning and an experienced builder who understands the unique challenges.

**Flood zones and setbacks** are the first considerations. Many Guadalupe River properties fall within FEMA-designated flood zones, which dictate minimum finished floor elevations and can affect insurance costs. Kerr County and the surrounding jurisdictions enforce specific setback requirements from the riverbank. Before purchasing a riverside lot, it is essential to review the flood maps and understand exactly where you can build and at what elevation. We work with surveying and engineering firms to establish these parameters early so there are no surprises.

**Foundation design** takes on added importance near water. Elevated foundations, pier-and-beam construction, and reinforced concrete slabs with proper moisture barriers are common approaches depending on the specific site conditions. Soil near rivers tends to have higher clay content and moisture variability, which can cause movement in a poorly designed foundation. A geotechnical report is not optional -- it is mandatory.

**Erosion control and stormwater management** protect both your investment and the river ecosystem. Properly designed drainage, vegetated buffer zones, and stabilized slopes prevent runoff from carrying sediment into the water. Native riparian plantings like bald cypress, sycamore, and river fern stabilize banks naturally while enhancing the beauty of the setting.

**The lifestyle payoff is extraordinary.** Imagine coffee on your porch overlooking a quiet stretch of the Guadalupe, kayaking from your own dock, or watching your grandchildren play in a clear limestone swimming hole steps from your back door. River properties deliver a quality of life that is difficult to match.
