# Energy-Efficient Features Every Luxury Home Should Have

There is a misconception that luxury homes and energy efficiency are at odds with each other. In reality, the best custom homes integrate high-performance building science with beautiful design. The result is a home that is more comfortable, quieter, and significantly less expensive to operate month after month.

Insulation is the foundation of an energy-efficient home, and in the Hill Country, spray foam insulation is the gold standard. We use closed-cell spray foam in exterior walls and open-cell in rooflines to create a continuous thermal envelope. This eliminates the gaps and voids common with traditional batt insulation and can reduce heating and cooling costs by thirty to fifty percent compared to code-minimum construction. Combined with advanced air sealing techniques, a well-insulated home stays comfortable even when temperatures outside climb past 100 degrees.

Windows are another critical component. We specify low-E coated, argon-filled windows with thermally broken frames as a baseline. For west-facing glass, we often upgrade to triple-pane or use high-performance tinting to manage solar heat gain. The right windows let you enjoy panoramic Hill Country views without turning your living room into a greenhouse every afternoon.

HVAC system design deserves more attention than most homeowners realize. A properly sized, zoned system with variable-speed compressors will outperform an oversized conventional unit in both comfort and efficiency. We work with mechanical engineers to perform Manual J load calculations for every home, ensuring the equipment matches the actual thermal demands of the structure rather than relying on rules of thumb.

Solar readiness is increasingly standard in our builds. Even if you choose not to install panels immediately, pre-wiring the electrical panel and reinforcing the roof structure for future solar costs very little during construction and saves thousands if you add panels later.
