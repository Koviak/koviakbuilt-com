## Building Science: An Integral Part of Construction

Building science is not a separate entity or an afterthought in construction; it's integrated into every aspect of building. From the moment two boards are nailed together, building science principles come into play, influencing the drying potential, thermal properties, and overall performance of the construction.

## The Myth of 'Starting to Use Building Science'

A common misconception is that building science is something builders and architects start using at some point. The truth is, if you're building, you're already dealing with building science. Every design decision, material choice, and construction technique is inherently influenced by building science principles.

## Understanding Basic Building Science Principles

At its core, building science examines how various elements of a building interact with each other and the environment. This includes understanding how heat, air, and moisture move through a building assembly. For example, knowing that heat moves from hot to cold is fundamental in designing effective insulation and ventilation strategies.

## Practical Applications of Building Science

Building science is evident in practical applications such as water management strategies, thermal control, and vapor barriers. It governs decisions like choosing the right sheathing for an exterior wall or determining the appropriate insulation for a specific climate.

## Integrating Building Science in Design

As an architect or builder, integrating building science into your designs is essential. It's not just about 'adding on' building science elements; it's about making informed decisions from the outset. This could mean prioritizing mechanical closets for HVAC systems with the same emphasis as other critical house components like kitchens or bathrooms.

## Building Science in Remodeling

Even in remodeling, building science plays a crucial role. Understanding why certain areas of a house may have issues like rot or moisture buildup can provide valuable insights into how to improve the building's performance during renovations.

## Educating Homeowners and Clients

Part of our role as builders and architects is to educate our clients on the importance of building science. Explaining the reasoning behind certain design choices or construction techniques can help clients understand the value of building science in their projects.

## Conclusion

Building science is everywhere in construction. It's an understanding that should be integrated into every decision we make. As we continue this Master Class series, we'll delve deeper into specific aspects of building science, demonstrating how it shapes and improves the buildings we create.
