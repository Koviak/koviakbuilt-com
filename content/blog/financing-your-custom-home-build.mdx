# Financing Your Custom Home Build

Financing a custom home is different from getting a mortgage on an existing property, and understanding the process upfront eliminates confusion and delays. The good news is that construction lending has become more accessible in recent years, and several local and regional banks serve the Hill Country market well.

**The construction loan** is the primary financing vehicle for custom homes. Unlike a traditional mortgage where you receive the full loan amount at closing, a construction loan disburses funds in stages -- called "draws" -- as the project progresses. Your lender will typically require an appraisal based on the plans and specifications, and draws are released after inspections confirm that work has been completed according to schedule. During construction, you typically pay interest only on the amount that has been disbursed, which keeps your carrying costs manageable.

**Most construction loans convert automatically** to a permanent mortgage once the home is complete. This "construction-to-permanent" structure means you only go through one closing, saving you thousands in duplicate closing costs. Interest rates on construction loans are generally slightly higher than conventional mortgage rates, but they apply only during the build period -- usually ten to fourteen months.

**What lenders want to see:** A strong credit profile, a down payment of typically twenty to twenty-five percent, detailed plans and specifications, a fixed-price contract with your builder, and proof that the builder carries appropriate insurance and licensing. Having these items organized before your first meeting with the lender accelerates the approval process significantly.

**Land equity counts.** If you already own your lot, many lenders will credit the land value toward your down payment. If the lot has appreciated since you purchased it, you may find that your equity position is stronger than you expected.

We recommend starting the financing conversation early -- ideally during the design phase. This allows you to align your budget with your floor plan and finish selections before breaking ground, which prevents the stress of discovering a gap between your vision and your borrowing capacity midway through the project.
