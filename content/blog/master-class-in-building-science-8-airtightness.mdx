## The Essence of Airtightness

### 1. Understanding Airtightness

Airtightness in buildings is about controlling air movement in and out of the structure. It's an integral part of building science, ensuring that we maintain comfortable indoor environments and energy efficiency.

### 2. The Importance of Continuity

Continuity is the backbone of airtightness. It refers to the uninterrupted air barrier system of a building. Imagine air tightness as a leak in a boat - you want no leaks, not even a small one. This principle guides our construction process, ensuring every component contributes to the overall airtightness.

### 3. The Blower Door Test

This test measures a building's airtightness and is a standard part of building codes in many areas. It involves sealing the building and using a fan to pressurize or depressurize the space, then measuring the air leakage. The lower the air leakage, the better the airtightness.

## Practical Steps for Achieving Airtightness

### 1. Addressing Penetrations and Joints

We carefully seal all penetrations and joints in the building envelope. This includes areas where different materials meet, such as where the foundation connects to walls or around windows and doors.

### 2. Selecting the Right Materials

Choosing the right materials is crucial. For instance, we use high-performance tapes and sealants to ensure that every joint and seam is properly sealed, contributing to the overall airtightness.

### 3. Design Considerations

We also pay close attention to design elements that impact airtightness. This includes the placement of windows, doors, and other potential air leakage points.

## The Role of Building Science in Airtightness

Building science provides the knowledge and tools to understand and control air movement in buildings. By applying these principles, we ensure that our buildings are not just structurally sound but also energy-efficient and comfortable.
